# Application Information
This page provides overview on the steps for each scholarship.

Keep in mind that the Ministry-funded scholarships may have different application processes, namely the "Embassy Recommendation", which is the staple application process for the undergraduate, special training and school of technology programs; and the "University Recommendation" route.

# Undergraduate

1. **Documentation Phase**: The first phase of the scholarship application. You send your documents and pray.

2. **Examination Phase**: Depending on your major, you'll have to undergo a different set of examinations.
    - **For Humanities majors**: Japanese, English, and Mathematics A; 
    - **For STEM majors**: Japanese, English, Mathematics B, Physics, and Chemistry (or biology); 
    - **For Medicine majors**: Japanese, English, Mathematics B, Physics, and Biology.

3. **Interview**: The interview stage is mostly a basic interview, with questions such as:
    - What field of study/ major would you like to study in Japan?
    - Why do you want to study in Japan, in specific.
    - Have you ever lived alone?
    - Do you want to work in Japan, or do you have plans to come back to your home country?
    - Do you know anything about life in Japan?

4. **Health Check**: If you pass the interview, congratulations, you have passed the **First Screening**. You can take a look at the previous year's documents [here](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1A_vOfY6rnmPx-A-vV8bkjp6w4czdf7RB?usp=drive_link).

5. **Global Phase**: The last stage. The longing is eternal. Time seems to pass slower. You refresh your email provider every couple of minutes. Nothing. Passing this phase grants you the scholarship.

# Research:
Courtesy of: @tachi.ttch

1. **BEFORE ANYTHING**: Write your research plan MONTHS in advance. You should start writing this at least around early January, don’t leave it for last! The whole Research category revolves around how good your research plan is! Try to showcase your repertoire and make it as perfect as you can, don’t even think about using AI or copying any parts of someone else’s work! They can test it using Turnitin, and you’ll be eliminated.

2. **Documentation Phase**: The first phase of the scholarship application. You send your documents and pray to have a profile strong enough to be called to the next phase.

3. **Examination Phase**:  Japanese and English test. (They will consider your highest score on the evaluation, so being intermediate on both is not an advantage; you need to be VERY GOOD at one of them, but you won’t pass if you get 100/100 for English and a 0/100 for Japanese, you still need to study both)

4. **Interview**: The interview stage can change a lot depending on your consulate/embassy, but there are a few standard questions you should expect. Remember to be coherent with the answers you wrote on the application form:
    - Why do you want to study in Japan? 
    - What’s your motivation to choose Japan, specifically?
    - Have you ever lived alone or abroad?
    - Do you want to work in Japan, or do you have plans to come back to your home country?
    - Do you know anything about life in Japan or Japanese culture?

> :information_source: You’ll also get some very specific questions about your research plan, so be ready to defend your point of view, your proposed methodology, why your research is relevant, what good it brings to your home country and Japan, and more! Don’t go there with vague notions and a research idea that anyone can pull off; be specific, show that you have real knowledge!

If you pass the interview, congratulations, **you have passed the First Screening!**.

5. **Health Check:** You can take a look at the previous year's documents [here](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1ShzxNw-9eM9AdmDJYKly0FiZgKnj5BXJ?usp=sharing). Find a doctor you trust to ask for physical exams and fill it out!

6. **Letter of Provisional Acceptance (LoPA):** This stage is also very crucial! You’ll receive a document from your local embassy/consulate showing all the universities you can apply for, search online, and really dive deep into the official university websites. Most labs also have their website, look into their current line of research, and available professors! 

> :information_source: After choosing, you’ll contact the professors by e-mail saying who you are, that you passed 1st screening for mext embassy recommendation, and what your research plan is. All your STAMPED documents should be attached to the e-mail; you can only contact TWO professors at a time, and only when refused or unanswered for a few days, you can move to the next. You can only get TWO LoPA, even if on the placement form you’ll choose 3 options!

> :warning:  For some universities (UTokyo, for example), you don’t contact the professor directly and instead apply through some kind of internal website; however, I recommend still contacting the professor you choose and letting them know you’re interested and about your research before applying on the university's website!

> :information_source: If you get at least ONE LoPA, congratulations, you now have a 99% chance of getting the scholarship! Fill in the Placement Preference Form. I recommend putting 1st and 2nd choice universities you got the LoPA and 3rd choice, some other university you think you’ll have a high chance of getting in.

> :warning: Remember that MEXT prefers that you choose national/imperial universities over private ones, even if you put a private university as your 1st choice, they might still send you to a national one!

7. **Global Phase (2nd Screening)**: It takes foreeeeeeeever to hear back from mext after you send the last forms and LoPA around September. January finally arrives and you refresh your email every couple of minutes. Nothing. Passing this phase grants you the scholarship (but for the Research category it is unheard of to get disqualified at this stage if you have a LoPA, maybe very few cases involving personal affairs, such as sickness or accidents). When the wait is over, you'll receive the final result for which university you’ll be going to, file the documents for the student visa application, which should be paid by your embassy, and FINALLY go to Japan!
